How to Read Patterns

Knitting and crohet patterns often use terms and abbreviations to explain what you’re supposed to do,rather than writing it out completely.

How to read Knitting Patterns

K or k = knit stitch

P or p = purl stitch

Abreviation Term Meaning
CO Cast on How you begin each knited piece
BO Bind off How you end a knitted piece(cast off)
Inc Increase Add a stitch.
Dec Decrease Eliminate a stitch.
Rep Repeat Do what you have been doing again for number listed in the pattern
Sl Slip Stich Slide the next stitch onto your right needle without working on it.
YO Yarn Over Wrap the yarn around the needle
Tog Together Work two or more stitches together.

How to read Crochet Patterns

Abreviation Meaning
Ch Chain Stitch
Sl st Slip Stitch
Sc Single Crochet Stitch
Hdc Hal- Stitch
Dc Double Crochet Stitch
Tc or trc Triple Crochet Stitch
Abreviation Meaning
Inc Increase/add one or more stitches.
Dec Decrease/Get rid one or more stitches.
Turn Turn your work aond so you can work on the next row.
Join Join 2 stitches together, usualy done with a slip stitch.
Rep Repeat what you did.

Common Symbols

Astericks

Astericks are used to show a sequence that is to be repeated. When anthing is written in astericks, you repeat that sequece until you reach wherever is specified in the pattern. This is usually until you reach the end of the row&bquo; until the certain amount of stitches remains, or until you reach a marker, depending on what the pattern specifies.

Parenthesis

Patterns usually have modifications for larger sizes written in parentheses. The pattern’s default is the smallest size, so if you require a larger size you would have to look at the number in the parenthesis to make your piece correctly.
